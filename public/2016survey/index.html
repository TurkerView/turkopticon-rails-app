Report for Turkopticon Community Survey

Thank you all for your patience with us! 1068 of you filled out the 2016 Turkopticon Community Survey.

First, why the delay? We had some trouble with SurveyGizmo, the service we used to host the survey. It took a few weeks to unbreak our account. Sorry about that!

Our hope was that the survey would have a couple of purposes.

First, it is a chance to communicate and reflect as a community. We compiled a summary report of responses so you can see how others feel about how Turkopticon is going. We also selected anonymous quotes from the open-ended questions that represent a range of insights, appreciations, and concerns expressed by the community. Some people love apples, and other people hate apples. But lots of people agree that fruit is important.

Second, the survey helps us understand the impact and intensity of issues. Some problems affect lots of people a bit. Other problems affect a few people a lot. One is not necessarily more important than the other. But it is helpful to know the difference. The survey helped us understand the difference.

We invite you to join us for a community call to talk about the results, ask questions, or discuss what is next. The call will be by voice and webchat. (Details to follow.) 150 of you told us yo are willing to help out. This call can be a place to discuss, suggest, and collaborate on small projects to improve Turkopticon.

Some of the top things we learned:
  • 78.8% of you are satisfied with how Turkopticon is going. There are many things that could be improved, but there seems to be a lot of good here despite conflicts and drama that can flare up.
  • A range of things people said points to the issue of what counts as a good review and who decides. Numerical ratings like fairness ask reviewers for their opinion, but some people want fairness standardized so they know how to interpret the rating. Some people want to explain the details of their experiences. But newbies get confused about which details are okay to reveal. Completely well-intentioned contributions and ideas about what makes a good review can cause lots of conflict.
  • We take this as a mandate to make a new review form that separates objectively reportable data about a HIT from subjective assessments of a HIT. We hear you that we need both. One worker even suggested a brilliant idea for an unmoderated question: "Would you recommend this requester to others?"
  • Another issue that came up across many responses was that of how to spread the burden of moderation more widely. The new review form should make moderation a lot easier. Once the form is in place, we can talk as a community about what moderation, community management, and newbie education should look like in the future.
  • We would like to make the survey an annual practice. A lot of people said they were happy we were doing it. It is one step in making Turkopticon something more decentralized, more worker governed, and something more people can actively have a hand in shaping.

Lilly and Six
Turkopticon Admins
